Catfish Recipe

Description Fried catfish is a tasty savory dish best served with fries.
    4 fillets catfish a little over a pound, total 1½ cups oil vegetable, canola or peanut oil (you may need more or less; depends on how deep your skillet is) 2 cups cornmeal yellow 1 cup buttermilk 1 tablespoon cayenne pepper (red pepper, ground) 1 teaspoon salt 1 teaspoon black pepper
  1. In a large bowl, add cornmeal, salt, pepper and cayenne; stir with fork till blended 2 cups cornmeal, 1 teaspoon salt, 1 teaspoon black pepper, 1 tablespoon cayenne pepper
  2. Using a cast-iron, or heavy bottom skillet, pour oil into skillet and heat to medium; 1½ cups oil
  3. While oil is heating, take a fillet directly from the buttermilk and place it in the cornmeal; pat the cornmeal on the fillet; coat it all over, twice; repeat with remaining fillets
  4. Check your skillet; the oil needs to be around 312 - 350ºF; you can sprinkle a few pieces of cornmeal in the oil to see if it sizzles; if it does sizzle, the oil is ready
  5. Cooking in batches of two fillets, place them in the hot oil; DO NOT MOVE THE FILLETS until they've cooked for 2 1/2 - 3 minutes on one side
  6. After 2 1/2 -3 minutes, use tongs and flip the fish over and cook for an additional 3 minutes
  7. Use a thermometer to measure internal temperature; it should be 145ºF
  8. Remove fillets from oil and place on a paper towel
  9. Repeat until all fillets are cooked and drained
  10. Plate fillets and serve with lemon and tartar sauce
